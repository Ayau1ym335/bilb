# Sustainability Standards for Historic Building Renovation

## Embodied Carbon: Renovation vs Demolition

### Key Numbers
- New construction embodied carbon: 800–1200 kg CO₂/m²
- Adaptive reuse embodied carbon: 300–500 kg CO₂/m²
- Net saving: **40–65% fewer emissions**
- Operational carbon (HVAC, lighting): roughly equal after renovation

### BILB Formula
E_impact = (M_brick × K_CO₂) + (M_transport × K_fuel)
- K_CO₂ (brick): 0.24 kg CO₂/kg of new brick manufactured
- K_CO₂ (concrete): 0.18 kg CO₂/kg
- K_fuel (transport): 0.062 kg CO₂/(tonne·km)
- Typical demolition waste: 1.2 tonnes/m² of floor area

## LEED for Existing Buildings (v4)
Historic buildings qualify for LEED EB O+M with specific credits:
- **MR Credit: Building and Material Reuse** — up to 5 points
- **EA Prerequisite: Energy Efficiency** — existing shell counts toward baseline
- **SS Credit: Site Assessment** — historic context documented

Targeting LEED Silver (50–59 pts) is realistic for most adaptive reuse projects.
LEED Gold (60–79 pts) achievable with major MEP upgrade.

## ICOMOS Principles (Cultural Heritage)
- Minimal intervention: do only what is necessary
- Reversibility: new works should be removable without damaging original fabric
- Authenticity: maintain materials, craftsmanship, and design intent
- Documentation: record all changes before and during works

## ESG Investment Criteria
**Environmental**: CO₂ savings quantified → emissions reduction certification
**Social**: Job creation, community cultural asset, affordable workspace
**Governance**: UNESCO/ICOMOS compliance, transparent reporting

ESG-rated adaptive reuse projects access:
- Green bonds at 0.2–0.5% lower interest
- Impact investment funds (Blackrock, Vanguard ESG, local development banks)
- EU Taxonomy-aligned financing (Article 9 funds)

## Kazakhstan / Central Asia Context
- State Program "Digital Kazakhstan" includes heritage digitization grants
- Akimat (city administration) cultural preservation funds: up to 30% subsidy
- Asian Development Bank: Urban Development loans for heritage projects
- UNDP Kazakhstan: Heritage tourism development grants

## Carbon Equivalences (for Reports)
- 1 tonne CO₂ saved = 45 trees growing for 1 year
- 1 tonne CO₂ saved = 4,000 km driven in average car
- 1 tonne CO₂ saved = 120 kg of coal not burned
