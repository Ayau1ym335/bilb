# Adaptive Reuse: Principles & Methodology

## Definition
Adaptive reuse is the process of repurposing an existing building for a new function
while preserving its architectural and historical character. It is the primary
alternative to demolition and new construction.

## Why It Matters (Economic Case)
- Adaptive reuse costs 16–25% less than equivalent new construction
- Construction timeline is 50% shorter (no foundation, shell already exists)
- Historic buildings command 15–25% rental premium vs comparable new builds
- Tax credits in most jurisdictions: 20% Historic Tax Credit (US), similar schemes in EU and CIS

## Primary Scenario Types

### 1. Cultural Hub / Creative Coworking
**Best fit**: High ceilings (≥3.5m), exposed brick, open floor plans, good natural light
**Target users**: Creative agencies, tech startups, artists, NGOs
**Revenue model**: Flexible desk rental ($200–600/desk/month), event space ($500–3000/event)
**Key works needed**: Sound insulation, electrical upgrade, HVAC, internet infrastructure
**Grant potential**: High — cultural heritage funds, municipal creative economy programs
**Timeline**: 12–18 months from decision to opening
**Risks**: Noise complaints from residential neighbors, fire code compliance

### 2. Boutique Hotel / Aparthotel
**Best fit**: Central location, distinctive facade, minimum 800 m² total area
**Revenue model**: $120–350/night; 60–75% occupancy = strong ROI
**Key works needed**: Full MEP replacement, fire suppression, elevator (if >3 floors), ADA compliance
**Timeline**: 18–30 months
**Risks**: High capex, complex permitting, operational complexity
**ESG angle**: Heritage tourism growing 12% YoY; "experiential travel" premium demographic

### 3. STEAM Education Center / Makerspace
**Best fit**: Industrial aesthetic, large floor plates, near university or tech cluster
**Revenue model**: Long-term institutional contracts (3–10 years), government grants
**Key works needed**: Lab ventilation, electrical capacity, accessibility
**Grant potential**: Very high — Ministry of Education, EU Horizon, USAID programs
**Timeline**: 14–20 months
**Risks**: Lowest financial risk; dependency on single institutional tenant

### 4. Mixed-Use Residential (Loft Apartments)
**Best fit**: Residential neighborhood, >500 m², good natural light
**Revenue model**: Sale or long-term rental; loft premium 20–40% vs standard apartments
**Key works needed**: Acoustic separation, kitchen/bath installation per unit, fire compartmentalization
**Timeline**: 20–36 months
**Risks**: Most complex permitting; zoning change may be required

## Feasibility Scoring Methodology
Score each factor 0–25; total 0–100:
- **Structural integrity**: 0 (total replacement needed) to 25 (minor repairs)
- **Location & access**: 0 (isolated, no transit) to 25 (central, pedestrian traffic)
- **Zoning flexibility**: 0 (heavily restricted) to 25 (mixed-use zone)
- **Market demand**: 0 (saturated market) to 25 (strong unmet demand)

Feasibility ≥75: Proceed; 50–74: Proceed with conditions; <50: Reconsider

## Common Issues → Recommended First Works
| Issue | Priority Action |
|-------|----------------|
| HIGH_HUMIDITY | Waterproofing membrane + drainage + dehumidification system |
| VIBRATION_DETECTED | Structural audit of connections and foundations |
| STRUCTURAL_RISK_COMBO | Emergency structural assessment before any other works |
| CRITICAL_STRUCTURAL_TILT | Geotechnical survey + underpinning assessment |
| TILT_DETECTED | Monitoring sensors + annual structural review |
| POOR_DAYLIGHTING | Skylight installation or facade glazing expansion |
| HIGH_TEMPERATURE | HVAC audit + insulation assessment |
